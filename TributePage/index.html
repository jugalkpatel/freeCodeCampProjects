Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India"

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Here's time line of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born on October 15, 1931, at Rameshwaram.

  • 1954 - Dr Kalam graduated from St. Joseph's in Trichy.

  • 1958 - Specialized in aero-engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.

  • 1960 - he joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation. Five years later he joined the Indian Space Research Organiasation.

  • 1980 - India enters the space club by putting the Rohini satellite in the near orbit with the first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), Developed under the stewardship of Dr. Kalam.

  • 1981 - Padma Bhushan for Dr. Kalam

  • 1982 - He joined the Defence Research and Development Laboratory in Hyderabad as director after his stint with the Vikram Sarabbai Space Centre.

  • 1987 - Om Prakash Bhasin awards for science and technology for 1986.

  • 1990 - Speaking on the 'Agni Mission' he said India's successful testing of the 'Agni' missile signified its expertise in the rarefied hi-tech areas of heat-shield technology Lok Sabha congratulated the Indian scientists team headed by Dr A. P. J. Abdul Kalam for the successful launch of the "Akash" multi-target surface-to-air missile. Padma Vibhushan for Dr. Kalam.

  • 1992 - Govermnent announced the appointment of Dr A P J Abdul Kalam as Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister, Secretary to the Department of Defence Research and Development and Director-General, Defence Research and Development Organization.

  • 1993 - The medium range surface-to-surface missile 'Prithvi' was launched from the interim test range (ITR) at Chandipur-on-Sea. Dr. Kalam was the Prithvi Project Director.

  • 1994 - Government has extended the tenure of the scientific adviser Dr. Kalam.

  • 1998 - Kalam Conferred Bharat Ratna Award. Sonia Gandhi conferred the Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration on eminent scientist A P J Abdul Kalam for his contribution to the development of strategic technology in the country.

  • 1999 - Wings of Fire: An Autobiography by A P J Abdul Kalam, Arun Tiwari; Universities Press

  • 2000 - Dr A P J Abdul Kalam conferred doctor of science degree by the Madurai Kamaraj University.

  • 2002 - A.P.J. ABDUL Kalam sworn in as the country's 12th President.

  • 2007 - His term lasted from 25 July 2002 to 25 July 2007.

  • 1931 - Died at the age of 83.
